Help & FAQ

Everything you need to know about using Phyto Lab for accurate plant disease diagnosis.

Getting Started

camera_alt

Step 1: Capture Image

Take a clear, well-lit photo of the affected plant leaf or area. Ensure the image shows the symptoms clearly.

  • • Use natural daylight
  • • Focus on the diseased area
  • • Avoid blurry or dark images

Step 2: Edit Image (Optional)

Use our built-in image editor to adjust brightness, contrast, rotation, and cropping for optimal analysis.

  • • Real-time preview of changes
  • • Skip editing if image is already good
  • • Enhances AI diagnosis accuracy

Step 3: Upload & Analyze

Apply your edits and upload the image. Our AI will analyze it instantly with improved accuracy.

  • • Supported formats: PNG, JPG, JPEG, GIF
  • • Maximum size: 16 MB
  • • Results in under 3 seconds

Frequently Asked Questions

What diseases can Phyto Lab detect?

Phyto Lab can identify over 38 different plant diseases across multiple crops including apples, corn, tomatoes, potatoes, and grapes. Our AI is trained on extensive datasets to recognize both common and rare conditions.

How accurate is the diagnosis?

Our models achieve 99% accuracy on validated test datasets. However, accuracy can vary based on image quality and disease stage. We recommend confirming critical diagnoses with laboratory testing.

Is my data secure?

Yes, uploaded images are processed securely and temporarily stored. We do not share or sell your data. Images are automatically deleted after processing unless you choose to save results.

What if I get an incorrect diagnosis?

AI diagnosis is a powerful tool but not infallible. If results don't match your observations, consider re-uploading with better lighting or different angles. For critical decisions, consult agricultural experts.

Can I use Phyto Lab on mobile devices?

Absolutely! Phyto Lab is fully responsive and works on smartphones, tablets, and desktops. Mobile users can take photos directly in the field for instant analysis.

What is the image preprocessing feature?

Our new image editor allows you to enhance your plant photos before analysis. You can adjust brightness, contrast, rotate images, and crop to focus on specific areas. This helps improve diagnosis accuracy by optimizing image quality.

How do I use the image editor?

After selecting or capturing an image, the editor modal appears automatically. Use the sliders and buttons to make adjustments, then click "Apply Changes & Upload" or "Skip Editing" to proceed with analysis.

Troubleshooting

Upload Issues

  • Unsupported format: Ensure your image is PNG, JPG, JPEG, or GIF
  • File too large: Compress or resize images under 16 MB
  • Network error: Check your internet connection and try again

Image Quality Tips

  • Lighting: Use bright, even natural light
  • Focus: Ensure the diseased area is sharp and clear
  • Angle: Photograph leaves from multiple angles if needed
  • Background: Plain backgrounds help AI focus on symptoms
